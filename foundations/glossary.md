# Glossary

This glossary defines the key terms used across the Simpl programme and this documentation catalogue. Definitions are sourced from the official Simpl programme glossary and represent the authoritative vocabulary for Simpl-Open and its data space ecosystem.

---

**Access policy**
Rules and conditions controlling how a Provider manages service operations, permissions, access rules, and selection rules to restrict or allow certain access to a service.

**Applicant**
An entity submitting an application to join, partake in, or receive something from a data space, typically at an initial engagement stage seeking approval based on data space governance criteria.

**Application catalogue**
A catalogue containing descriptions of applications such as services, tools, and workflows provided within a data space.

**Authentication provider federation**
A federation of authentication service providers enabling End-Users to authenticate seamlessly across different systems or organisations using a unified, interoperable framework.

**Catalogue**
A centralised repository or directory storing and organising descriptions of assets (data, applications, infrastructure) to enable discovery by Participants.

**Consent**
"Any freely given, specific, informed and unambiguous indication of the data subject's wishes by which they, by a statement or by a clear affirmative action, signifies agreement to the processing of personal data relating to them."

**Consumer**
A Participant accessing data, applications, and infrastructure from a data space who can search, use online, or download resources if policy allows.

**Contract clause**
Defines terms and conditions for using an asset and the Provider's commitments, including service level agreements, license agreements, and billing schemas.

**Contract clause — Billing schema**
Defines how service usage is tracked, priced, and billed, covering pricing models, consumption units, and billing cycles.

**Contract clause — Service level agreement**
Defines expected performance and quality standards of a service, including metrics such as uptime, response time, and support provisions.

**Contract clause — Licence**
Defines terms under which a resource may be used, including allowed uses, usage restrictions, and intellectual property rights.

**Contract issuer**
The entity or role creating and issuing contract offerings to another party, typically the Provider organisation or its authorised Representative.

**Data catalogue**
A catalogue containing descriptions of data resources or services such as data assets, schemas, metadata, and access policies provided within a data space.

**Data orchestration platform**
A system coordinating, managing, and automating data workflows through scheduling, execution, monitoring, and governance of data processing tasks.

**Data policy**
A set of rules, working instructions, preferences, and guidance ensuring data is obtained, stored, retrieved, and manipulated consistently with governance framework standards.

**Data rights holder**
A party holding legal rights and/or obligations to use, grant access to, or share personal or non-personal data, with ability to transfer such rights.

**Data sharing agreement**
A formal contract between parties defining how data will be shared and used, outlining permissions, restrictions, and responsibilities for compliance.

**Data space**
A distributed system with a governance framework enabling secure and trustworthy data transactions between Participants while supporting trust and data sovereignty.

**Digital signer**
An extended role identifying End-Users able to digitally sign on behalf of a Participant, lending legal validity and making contracts tamper-proof and verifiable.

**End-User**
An ultimate user of the Simpl-Open agent, either a real person interacting via user interface or an IT system interacting via agent APIs.

**Governance Authority**
The data space Participant accountable for creating, developing, operating, maintaining, and enforcing the governance framework for a particular data space.

**Governance framework**
The set of principles, standards, policies, agreements, and practices applying to governance, management, and operations of a data space and enforcement thereof.

**Governance policy**
A policy defining the overall governance framework including decision-making bodies, roles, responsibilities, change-control, and review cycles.

**Identity attribute**
A characteristic or information about an entity used to make access control decisions, such as organisation, role, or access level.

**Identity provider federation**
A collaborative arrangement among multiple identity providers mutually recognising and sharing user identities across different organisations or services.

**Infrastructure catalogue**
A catalogue containing descriptions of infrastructure resources or services such as compute, storage, and network offerings provided within a data space.

**Participant**
A party committed to a data space's governance framework with certain rights and obligations, either a Governance Authority, Provider, or Consumer.

**Policy**
Rules that Providers enforce to control access and usage of their resources.

**Provider**
A Participant operating resources and offering them to others by publishing those resources in catalogues for discovery and access.

**Representative**
A human End-User acting on behalf of a data space Participant, authorised to perform actions for their organisation.

**Resource description**
Metadata records describing various resources allowing identification, searchability, access management, preservation, and behaviour prediction.

**Schema**
The structure, format, and organisation of metadata describing resources in a data space catalogue, defining rules for how Providers must create resource descriptions.

**Security attribute provider federation**
A network of security attribute providers collaborating to share and manage security-related attributes of End-Users across different domains or services.

**Self-description**
A metadata record that Providers use to describe themselves, their data product offerings, and their resources and services.

**Semantic validation**
The process of ensuring data is meaningful and complies with predefined vocabularies, rules, and models defined within a specific context.

**Service contract**
A legal agreement governing the provision and consumption of a service between a Provider and Consumer, specifying deliverables and performance levels.

**Simpl-Open**
"An open source software stack that powers data spaces and other cloud-to-edge federations initiatives" focusing on leveraging open source community collaboration.

**Simpl-Open agent**
A set of integrated software components deployed by a Participant, acting as a local gateway for data and service interactions.

**Syntax validation**
The process of checking that a data structure or file conforms to required format and grammar rules such as JSON or XML syntax.

**Transaction**
The result of an interaction between two Participants with the purpose of sharing, accessing, or exchanging resources.

**Transaction Participant**
A data space Participant directly participating in a transaction by providing a resource, providing permissions/consent, or receiving/consuming resources.

**Usage contract**
A signed agreement between a Provider and Consumer stipulating the services offered and conditions all parties must adhere to.

**Usage contract offer**
An initial proposal created based on resource description information, outlining contract clauses, usage policies, and access policies for Consumer usage.

**Usage policy**
Policies defined by the Provider for resource usage in a data space, regulating permissible actions and behaviours related to accessed data/applications/infrastructure.

**Vocabulary**
A set of standardised, machine-readable terms including classes and properties used to model data semantics and enable consistent interpretation across domains and providers.

**Vocabulary management**
The governance and lifecycle of vocabularies within a data space, ensuring semantic interoperability of data structures across domains and providers.
